The Reality

At AlaanPay, I noticed a critical gap: users would clear all their onboarding and compliance steps, but stop right before making their first transaction with the card.

Users are naturally sticky with old habits and get lazy easily. Because there wasn’t a system intercepting them at the right moment, they just reverted to their legacy payment methods.

This exact friction is identical to what SPICE faces with "at-the-table leakage" where a diner sits down, but instead of opening the app to pay, they reflexively pull out a physical Mada or Apple Pay card and pay via the restaurant's hardware terminal.

How I Engineered The Loop

To get to a 100% activation rate, I didn't blast users with random messages. I split the solution into 3 highly timed touchpoints that bridged digital actions with physical reality.

Bringing This Architecture to SPICE

Solving the "at-the-table payment leakage" isn't a marketing problem. It’s a behavioral psychology challenge. Diners need a contextual "nudge" that breaks their muscle memory.

1. The Physical Space

Designing smart tabletop visuals and barcodes that make scanning feel easier and more rewarding than reaching for a wallet (just like the smart packaging code).

2. The Digital Trigger

Setting up geo-triggered, behavior-based in-app nudges the exact moment the waiter prints the check.

3. The Infrastructure

Building these automated retention journeys from absolute scratch using Insider, turning SPICE from a one-time trial into an unprompted dining habit.
